UNIT 2 PROJECT

curiosity cabinet

a collection of everyday questions people carry around quietly. click to open, read, and wander.

Why do we always remember something right after leaving the house?

Our brains often notice forgotten tasks once a routine is interrupted, which is why the missing thought shows up right after we step outside.

Why does food taste better when someone else makes it?

When we are not busy cooking, we experience the meal more fully. Surprise, care, and anticipation can also make food feel more satisfying.

Why do songs sound more emotional at night?

At night there are fewer distractions, so feelings become louder and music can feel more personal and intense.

Why do we open the fridge when we are not even hungry?

Sometimes we are looking for distraction, comfort, or routine rather than food. The fridge becomes a small place to check when we feel restless.

Why do we suddenly forget what we were about to say?

A small interruption or shift in attention can break the thread of a thought, making it disappear for a moment.

Why does a short nap sometimes feel better than a full night of sleep?

A short nap can feel refreshing because it gives the brain quick recovery without the heaviness that sometimes follows oversleeping.

Why do people look more attractive when you start liking them?

Attention, familiarity, and emotional attachment change perception. Once you feel connected, small details begin to look more beautiful.

Why does cleaning feel urgent when you need to do something else?

Cleaning can become a way to feel productive without facing a more difficult or stressful task directly.

Why do we reread old messages?

Old messages can hold comfort, memory, or unresolved feeling. Rereading them is sometimes a way of revisiting a past version of yourself.

Why does time go by faster during weekends?

When time feels limited and enjoyable, it often seems to move faster because we are more aware of it passing.

Why do we get attached to random objects?

Objects can carry memory, habit, or emotion. Even ordinary things start to matter when they become part of daily life.

Why does rain make some people feel calm?

The steady sound, softer light, and slower pace of rainy weather can make the world feel quieter and more contained.

Why do we always want snacks while watching something?

Watching and eating often become linked as a ritual, so the brain begins to expect both together.

Why do we miss people more at night?

Night creates stillness. Without daytime distractions, thoughts and emotions often become more noticeable.

Why does waiting for a text feel so dramatic?

Uncertainty makes time stretch. Even a small message can feel emotionally loaded when you care about the response.

Why do we laugh more when we are tired?

When we are tired, self-control weakens a little, so things can seem funnier and reactions become bigger.

Why does your room feel different after cleaning it?

Cleaning changes more than the space. It can shift your mood, attention, and sense of control, making the room feel emotionally lighter too.

Why do we keep clothes we never wear?

Clothes can represent who we were, who we want to be, or a version of ourselves we are not ready to let go of.

Why do we always think of the perfect reply too late?

Pressure in the moment can block thinking. Later, when the stress is gone, better words finally show up.

Why do familiar smells bring back memories so quickly?

Smell is closely tied to memory and emotion, so even a small scent can suddenly bring back a vivid moment.

Why do we scroll even when we are bored of scrolling?

Scrolling promises novelty with very little effort, so it becomes easy to keep going even after it stops feeling good.

Why do some people need background noise to focus?

A little sound can make a space feel less empty and can help block out more distracting thoughts or noises.

Why does water taste better at 3 a.m.?

When you are tired or half asleep, even something simple can feel strangely satisfying and dramatic.

Why do we feel productive after writing things down?

Writing down a thought can make it feel more organized and manageable, even before anything is finished.

Why do we romanticize small moments later?

Memory often softens ordinary experiences. Looking back can make simple moments feel more meaningful than they did at the time.
