UNIT 2 PROJECT

curiosity cabinet

a collection of everyday curiosities. open drawers and discover hidden emotions.

drawer 01
Why does rain feel calming?

Soft repetitive sounds help slow our nervous system and make the world feel quieter.

Why do quiet mornings feel special?

Less stimulation allows our thoughts to settle before the day becomes crowded.

Why do warm drinks feel comforting?

Warmth and routine can create a small feeling of safety and care.

Why do we enjoy sitting by windows?

Windows let us observe the world from a protected distance, which feels peaceful.

Why do slow days feel peaceful?

When time feels less pressured, even simple moments can feel softer and fuller.

drawer 02
Why do we wave back by accident?

Our social reflexes often act faster than our thinking in awkward public moments.

Why do we laugh when nothing is funny?

Nervous laughter can release tension when we do not know how else to respond.

Why do we replay conversations later?

The mind revisits uncertain moments and imagines better versions of them.

Why do we forget names immediately?

In social situations, attention often goes to anxiety and impression rather than memory.

Why do awkward silences feel so long?

When we feel responsible for filling the silence, even a few seconds stretch.

drawer 03
Why do old songs feel emotional?

Music can preserve a specific time, mood, or person more vividly than words.

Why do smells bring back memories?

Scent is deeply tied to memory and can return us to a moment almost instantly.

Why do we reread old messages?

Sometimes we revisit old words to reconnect with a feeling we are not ready to lose.

Why do childhood places feel smaller?

As we grow, our sense of scale changes, and memory no longer fills space the same way.

Why do we miss people more at night?

Without daytime noise, emotion has more room to rise to the surface.

drawer 04
Why do we open the fridge when not hungry?

We are often looking for novelty, comfort, or interruption rather than food.

Why do we google random things at night?

At night, the mind wanders more freely and small questions suddenly feel urgent.

Why do we click things just to see?

Curiosity rewards us for testing possibility, even before we know the answer.

Why do we get distracted while working?

The brain naturally seeks easier stimulation when focus begins to feel effortful.

Why do random thoughts appear suddenly?

Thoughts can surface from loose associations when the mind drifts.

drawer 05
Why do we smile when others smile?

People naturally mirror expressions, so another person’s smile can shift our own face.

Why does laughter feel better together?

Shared laughter creates connection, making joy feel larger than it does alone.

Why do small achievements feel satisfying?

Even tiny completions can create relief because effort finally feels visible.

Why do pets improve our mood?

Softness, affection, and simple presence can quickly reduce stress.

Why does sunlight feel so good?

Light affects mood, energy, and attention, making the body feel more open.
